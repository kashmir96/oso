CKF Second Brain — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 26 April 2026

CKF Second Brain (the "App") is a private personal-use application maintained by Curtis Fairweather, Christchurch, New Zealand, for personal goal tracking, journaling, reflection, and integration with health and fitness data. It is not a commercial product, has no other users, and is not offered or marketed to anyone else.

1. What data is collected

The App stores the following on behalf of the account owner only:

  • User-generated content: goals, diary entries, routine tasks, business tasks, AI conversation history, long-term memory facts.
  • Authentication credentials for the App itself (a hashed password and a session token).
  • OAuth tokens (access and refresh) for any third-party integrations the account owner connects.
  • Health and fitness metrics retrieved from connected services (e.g. Whoop): recovery score, HRV, resting heart rate, strain, sleep performance, sleep duration, sleep efficiency, workout summaries — all linked to the date they cover.

2. How data is used

Data is used solely to:

  • Display the account owner's own metrics and progress back to him.
  • Provide context to the App's AI assistant (Anthropic Claude) when generating responses, summaries, or recommendations the account owner has requested.
  • Track changes over time so progress against goals is visible.

Data is not used for advertising, profiling, training of third-party AI models, sale, or any commercial purpose.

3. Who has access

Data is accessible only to:

  • The account owner (the single authorised email address).
  • The infrastructure providers required to run the App, each operating under their own published privacy policies:
    • Supabase — database storage
    • Netlify — hosting and serverless functions
    • Anthropic — AI inference for the conversational assistant
    • OpenAI — speech-to-text (Whisper)
    • ElevenLabs — text-to-speech
    • Twilio — SMS reminders to a single hard-coded phone number

The App does not share data with any other party.

4. Third-party integrations

When the account owner connects a third-party service such as Whoop, he authorises the App to read the data covered by the OAuth scopes granted at the time of connection (recovery, sleep, cycle, workout, and profile data). Data flows directly from the third-party service to a private serverless function and into the private Supabase database. It is not redistributed. The account owner may revoke access at any time from the third-party service's own dashboard, or by disconnecting the integration from the App's settings, which deletes the stored tokens.

5. Storage and security

Data is stored in a private Supabase database with row-level security enabled, and is accessed only through authenticated server-side functions running on Netlify. OAuth tokens are stored encrypted at rest by the database provider. The App is gated to a single email address; any other login attempt is refused at the application layer.

6. Retention and deletion

Data is retained indefinitely unless the account owner deletes it. He may delete any record (goal, diary entry, message, memory fact, integration token, metric) directly from the App. Disconnecting an integration revokes its tokens and stops further data collection from that source. Deletion of the underlying database removes everything.

7. Children

The App is for the personal use of an adult and is not directed at children.

8. Changes to this policy

Updates to this policy will appear on this page with a revised "Last updated" date. Material changes affecting how connected third-party data is handled will be reflected here before any change in practice.
